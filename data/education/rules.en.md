# Rules for working with the system (portal)

A training page for the HR PRO AI recruiter. Based on the methodology of assessing productivity and personality, hiring factors, the six hiring steps, and the "Result" and "Tools" tests.

---

## 1. How the system works

The HR PRO AI system contains several independent tests. A **link** is sent to the candidate by email; they take the test online, and the result arrives in the recruiter's dashboard.

Core tests:
1. **"Result"** — a productivity/effectiveness test. 20 open-ended questions. The first step, a filter for results and adequacy.
2. **"Tools"** — a personality test, 200 questions, 10 indicators (points A–J). The second step.
3. Additional short tests: **intelligence (IQ)**, leadership, reproduction.

---

## 2. How to send tests to candidates (key rules)

**Follow the sequence of tests.** First — "Result" (results), then "Tools" (personality traits).
> "There is no point in testing personality traits in isolation from results."

If a person has no results, then no matter how "adorable" their personality test may be — the collaboration won't work out.

**Do not send both tests at once.** This disrupts how the system works. The logic is a funnel: first the easy "Result" test (quick), and only to candidates who spark interest — the large "Tools". If you send everything at once (over 200 questions, ~an hour of work), the candidate will be overloaded and "drop off". Two tests at once are acceptable only in very rare cases.

**Sometimes the second test can be skipped.** If the "Result" scores are excellent and there is full confidence that the candidate fits — "Tools" can be skipped. But the second test increases the level of detail when there are several candidates and a choice must be made.

**Always give feedback.** You cannot leave a person without a reply: they opened the email, spent 30–40 minutes on the test — they deserve at least a gentle rejection, and ideally — an invitation.
> "The worst thing is when you simply didn't reply at all."
Do not devalue the candidate and do not drag out the reply for weeks. Reply right away: "we liked your results, we'll look at a few more candidates and invite you for an interview." The system is monitored for "hanging" candidates who have been left without a reply.

**Confirm receipt of the email.** Emails with the link often end up in spam (email services conflict with one another and filter out emails from unfamiliar addresses and with links). If an interesting candidate did not receive the email — ask them to check the "Spam" folder.

**Do not "cut off" effective people based on the personality test.**
> "Do not cut off effective people if Tools seems suboptimal."
If a person has real results but their "Tools" chart looks less than ideal — that is no reason to reject them. You cannot, for example, say "you have low persistence, so you won't be able to sell": if they have already achieved results, their tools allow them to be effective.

---

## 3. How to read results and guide the candidate through the stages

**The "Result" test** gives the candidate's productivity type and a new "résumé in the format of results":
- **Winner (performer)** — inherently effective, sees and measures the product, takes responsibility, works without a "kick". Can be a generalist (effective everywhere) or a specialist (in one field).
- **Doer** — a good performer, loves the work and is not afraid of responsibility, but does not always see/formulate the end product; works excellently under a performer's direction.
- **Waiter (the one who waits)** — avoids work and responsibility, has no results.

Approximate proportion in the population: **~20% Winner, ~40% Doer, ~40% Waiter.**

**Actions in the dashboard based on the "Result" outcome:** "Send test 2 (Tools)", "Schedule an interview", "Reject" (a tactful automatic rejection).

**The "Tools" test** is read like a person's "operating manual": 10 indicators from −100 to +100, divided into zones (from very low to very high). It is a "photograph of the personality at a moment in time". Personality traits are a **negative indicator**: what is assessed is not "how good" but how much the character may get in the way in a role and within the team, and how to manage such a person more effectively.

**The funnel in practice:** from ~100 applications the tests narrow it down to ~20 tested, of whom 1–3 are hired.

## 4. Hiring factors and their priority

The candidate is assessed against four factors. The foundation beneath them is **reference checking** (verifying facts of the past: "who can confirm this", phone numbers), because the past is verifiable, whereas the future is not.

1. **Productivity** — the main factor, in a class of its own. The presence of benefit and results in the past and present. Innate; verified by a productivity interview + the "Result" test.
   > "If a person is not productive — they are of no interest under any circumstances."
2. **Personality traits** — the "Tools" test. We assess risk: how much the character gets in the way in the role and within the team.
3. **Motivation** — what drives the person. Valuable are those oriented toward the "outflow" (what a person gives), not only the "inflow" (what they receive). Scale from top to bottom: **Duty → Personal conviction → Personal gain → Money**.
4. **Knowledge** — the ability to confidently deliver the expected result in practice (not diplomas). Scale: **To know → Not to know (an honest stance) → "I think I know" (the worst)**.

**Priority of factors:**
> **Productivity > Personality traits > Motivation > Knowledge.**
> "Knowledge can be given, motivation can be raised a little, but productivity is innate and does not change."

That is why, in the system, productivity ("Result") is checked **first**, and only then — everything else.

---

## 5. The six steps in hiring

1. **Decide** — who is needed: draw up a portrait of the role.
2. **Attract** — a job posting (tailored to each role).
3. **Quickly process the flow** of candidates — filter by tests, **not by résumés**.
4. **Selection** — testing and interviews.
5. **Onboarding** — adaptation of the new employee.
6. **Strengthening the employee** — the "Tools" test as an "operating manual"; strengthen the strong.

The first steps (decide and attract) matter most: if you make a mistake on them, nothing further will save the situation.

**On onboarding (an important rule of exchange):** from the very first day a newcomer receives two inflows (salary + training) and often zero outflow — the exchange is disrupted, and "within 2–3 weeks a good employee becomes a critic". The rule: **train for half a day at most**, the other half — simple real work from the first second, so that the person immediately gets an outflow (benefit).

---

## 6. A short recruiter checklist

- [ ] "Result" first, then "Tools". Not both at once.
- [ ] Identified the type: Winner / Doer / Waiter.
- [ ] Gave feedback to everyone who took the test (even if a gentle rejection).
- [ ] Confirmed receipt of the email (spam check) for interesting candidates.
- [ ] Did not reject an effective candidate merely because of a "suboptimal" Tools.
- [ ] Assessed the candidate against the 4 factors in the correct priority.
- [ ] Filtered by tests, not by résumés.
